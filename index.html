PKOS: A Continuity Substrate Under Acceleration

Artificial intelligence accelerates the production of analysis, synthesis, and institutional action. What is not accelerating at the same pace is the infrastructure required to preserve meaning, responsibility, and justification.

Audited State introduces PKOS—a Persistent Semantic Scaffold designed for long-horizon human-AI collaboration. It is an empirical model OS built to test how institutions can scale complexity without losing their reconstructable reasoning.

The Structural Framework

The Problem

Interpretive entropy under AI scaling. As semantic surface area and temporal duration expand, original intent and operational interpretation gradually diverge.

The Threshold

Non-linear mutation velocity. The point at which unaided human cognition can no longer manage the blast radius of structural changes across dependent systems.

The Response

Persistent scaffolding + forward intent artifacts. An infrastructural layer that anchors definitions explicitly and preserves reconstructable reasoning lineage.

The Mechanism

Translation layer enabling shared coordinates. A substrate that structures mutation, allowing human and synthetic agents to operate across a unified authority membrane.

The Method

Measurable governance under stress. Continuous auditing of state shifts where memory acts as the highest court, allowing repair to replace rewrite.

The Invitation

Collaboration on empirical validation. Testing the boundaries of inspectable accumulation in real-world, high-velocity environments.

Core Concepts

To navigate interpretive stability, we must differentiate the symptoms of acceleration from the infrastructure required to manage it.

  • Interpretive Entropy: The gradual decay of shared meaning in long-term human-AI systems.
  • Semantic Surface Area (SSA): The expanding web of definitions, constraints, and dependencies that make up a system.
  • Reconstruction Cost: The cognitive and procedural effort required to understand why a past decision was made.
  • Epistemic Maturity: The structured evolution of ideas from perspective to durable commitment without coercive control.
