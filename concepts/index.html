Concepts

A glossary of the structural pressures and architectural principles defining the Audited State framework.

The glossary below introduces the structural vocabulary used throughout the framework. For an overview of how these concepts interact within the larger system, see the Concept Map, which illustrates how these pressures and architectural responses combine to preserve continuity as knowledge systems evolve.

The Physics of Scaling

Interpretive Entropy

The gradual divergence between original semantic intent and current operational interpretation within evolving institutional systems.

Semantic Surface Area

The total set of semantic anchors, cross-references, governance constraints, and operational dependencies defining an institutional system.

Reconstruction Cost

The cognitive and procedural effort required to reconstruct the reasoning lineage that produced a given institutional state.

Mutation Blast Radius

The extent to which a proposed semantic or structural change propagates across dependent anchors within the Semantic Surface Area.

Semantic Drift

The quiet unmooring of language and assumptions over time, amplified by the speed of automated generation.

Architectural Solutions

Persistent Semantic Scaffold (PSS)

A structured, addressable, mutation-governed semantic substrate designed to stabilize evolving institutional meaning.

Inspectable Accumulation

The capacity of a system to increase its complexity and surface area while preserving reconstructable reasoning.

Authority Membrane

The structural boundary through which lawful mutation must pass before altering canonical semantic state.

Epistemic Maturity

The structured evolution of ideas—from perspective to commitment—within a continuity-preserving architecture.

Foundational Principles

Continuity

The preservation of decision lineage across time. Without continuity, accountability diffuses into isolated outputs.

Human Agency

In automated systems, agency is not defined by generation, but by the assumption of responsibility at the point of decision.

Governance vs Control

Control dictates outputs; governance stewards the structural conditions under which outputs can be evaluated.

Constraint as Enabler

Structural boundaries do not limit action; they stabilize it, allowing acceleration without fragmentation.

Shadow AI

Unsanctioned, invisible delegation that optimizes local tasks while undermining institutional continuity.
