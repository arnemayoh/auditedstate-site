Persistent Semantic Scaffold (PSS)

A Persistent Semantic Scaffold (PSS) is a structured, addressable, mutation-governed semantic substrate designed to enable inspectable accumulation.

Infrastructure for Meaning

A PSS differs from version control systems, knowledge graphs, and policy engines by stabilizing the evolving institutional meaning itself. It functions as the core infrastructural layer for long-horizon human-AI collaboration by ensuring that:

  • Definitions are anchored explicitly
  • Reconstructable reasoning lineage is preserved
  • Incremental constitutional mutation is enabled
  • Authority-scoped validation is supported
  • Accumulation occurs without loss of inspection

The PKOS Implementation

PKOS operates as a prototype instantiation of this scaffold. It proves that beyond certain thresholds of complexity and institutional duration, such scaffolding transitions from an optional design preference to a structural necessity.
