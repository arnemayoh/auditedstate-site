State

State refers to the recorded condition of a decision at a specific moment in time. It preserves what was known, intended, validated, and accepted before action became durable.

Why State Matters

Acceleration increases the speed of revision. Outputs change rapidly. Assumptions evolve. Context shifts.

Without preserved state, reconstruction becomes interpretive rather than factual. Observers must guess what was believed or intended at the time.

State anchoring prevents this ambiguity.

What a State Snapshot Preserves

A durable state may include:

  • Declared intention
  • Authorship and delegation
  • Justified reasoning
  • Validated assumptions
  • Relevant context at time of decision

It does not preserve everything. It preserves what makes responsibility reconstructable.

State and Continuity

Continuity depends on memory. Memory depends on preserved state.

When state snapshots are visible, revision does not erase prior reasoning. It builds upon it.

Without state anchoring, semantic drift accelerates. Interpretations replace records.

State Is Not Surveillance

Preserving state is not equivalent to monitoring behavior. It applies only at the boundary of durable decision.

Exploration may remain fluid. Promotion into durable consequence requires anchoring.

State and Repair

Errors are inevitable. Repair requires the ability to revisit prior states without distortion.

A recorded state enables comparison:

  • What changed?
  • Why did it change?
  • Was the change justified?

Without preserved state, repair becomes speculative. With it, correction becomes disciplined.

Relationship to the Loop

State anchoring follows Promotion. It enables Audit and informed revision.
