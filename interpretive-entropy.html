Interpretive Entropy

Interpretive Entropy is the gradual divergence between original semantic intent and current operational interpretation within evolving institutional systems.

The Physics of Decay

Consider a long-horizon human-AI collaboration engaged in evolving an institutional system over months or years. Definitions are refined, constraints are added, and governance structures are adjusted.

Interpretive entropy emerges from:

  • Bounded reconstructive cognition
  • Increasing cross-referential complexity
  • Mutation history accumulation
  • Defensive simplification under load

Not a Human Failure

Interpretive entropy reflects structural scaling pressure rather than individual failure. It is a natural condition of complex systems stretched across time, amplified by the speed of AI generation.
