The Problem: Interpretive Entropy

Artificial intelligence accelerates the production of analysis, synthesis, and institutional action. What is not accelerating at the same pace is the human capacity to reconstruct the reasoning behind it.

The Vulnerability of Scaling

As long-horizon human-AI collaboration scales, the semantic surface area of a project expands. Definitions are refined, constraints are added, and cross-references multiply.

Initially, complexity remains manageable. But over time, revisiting a prior decision requires reconstructing not only the decision itself, but the entire reasoning context in which it was made.

Interpretive Entropy

We define this dynamic as Interpretive Entropy: the gradual divergence between original semantic intent and current operational interpretation within evolving institutional systems.

It emerges from bounded reconstructive cognition colliding with an accumulating history of mutations. This is not a result of individual failure or deception. It is a structural vulnerability caused by scaling pressure.

The Breaking Point: Reconstruction Cost

Every institutional system relies on the ability to answer: Why was this decided?

As semantic surface area expands, the cognitive and procedural effort required to reconstruct that reasoning lineage increases. When this Reconstruction Cost exceeds practical thresholds, collaboration faces a structural crisis. The typical responses are:

  • Defensive simplification to reduce cognitive load
  • Large-scale rewrites to restore coherence
  • Silent, gradual reinterpretation of prior intent

All three responses increase instability over time.

Opacity Without Malice

As systems become more seamless, the Mutation Blast Radius of small changes widens. Responsibility may remain human in principle, yet become impossible to trace in practice.

The structural question is not whether AI should be used. It already is. The question is whether we can build infrastructure capable of containing mutation impact and preserving reconstructable reasoning under acceleration.
