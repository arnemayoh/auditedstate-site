Concept Map

The Audited State framework describes how reasoning can remain continuous as knowledge systems accelerate under AI-assisted work.

The concepts in this framework fall into three roles:

  • System Pressures – forces that destabilize meaning as systems scale
  • Reasoning Practices – how humans and AI collaborate to explore ideas
  • Continuity Architecture – mechanisms that preserve reasoning across time

Together they form the PKOS reasoning cycle. 

  Entropy → Conjecture → Record → Scaffold → Continuity

In other words: pressures create instability, collaborative reasoning explores solutions, reasoning records preserve insight, and architectural constraints stabilize evolving knowledge systems.

System Pressures

As knowledge systems grow, several structural pressures naturally emerge.

  • Semantic Surface Area increases as concepts, rules, and dependencies accumulate.
  • Reconstruction Cost rises as it becomes harder to reconstruct the reasoning behind past decisions.
  • Interpretive Entropy emerges when operational interpretation diverges from original intent.
  • Semantic Drift gradually alters meaning across time.
  • Mutation Blast Radius describes how widely a change propagates through dependent structures.

These pressures are not failures of individuals or institutions. They are natural consequences of scaling complexity.

Reasoning Practice

When systems face interpretive pressure, solutions do not emerge automatically. They are developed through reasoning.

Within the PKOS framework, reasoning often takes the form of Collaborative Conjecture: an iterative dialogue between humans and AI systems in which hypotheses are explored, tested, and refined.

Key practices include:

  • Collaborative Conjecture – human–AI exploration of hypotheses
  • Assumption & Inference Review – surfacing hidden assumptions and reasoning steps
  • Inspectable Reasoning – ensuring reasoning can be reconstructed later

These practices generate structured reasoning artifacts that can carry insight forward through time.

Continuity Architecture

The framework introduces architectural mechanisms designed to stabilize evolving systems.

  • Persistent Semantic Scaffold maintains stable reference points for evolving concepts.
  • Inspectable Accumulation allows systems to grow while preserving reconstructable reasoning.
  • Authority Membrane establishes a boundary through which structural changes must pass before becoming canonical.
  • State Anchoring preserves snapshots of system state to enable comparison across time.

Together these mechanisms reduce interpretive entropy and preserve reasoning continuity.

The PIFR Continuity Spine

<

The operational core of the system is a sequence of Pay-It-Forward Records (PIFRs).

A PIFR captures the reasoning produced through collaborative conjecture and preserves it as a structured artifact that can be revisited, audited, and extended by future reasoning.

Each PIFR carries forward:

  • intent
  • reasoning context
  • references to prior records
  • criteria for evaluation
  • relevant state queries

Conceptually this forms a continuity spine:

PIFR₁ → PIFR₂ → PIFR₃ → PIFR₄

Each record extends the reasoning lineage of the system.

Human-in-the-Loop Checkpoints (HuLoo)

Every PIFR passes through HuLoo checkpoints, ensuring that responsibility remains visible and accountable.

Typical checkpoints include:

  • proposal
  • review
  • critique
  • ratification
  • promotion to canonical state

Automated tools may assist these processes, but the transition between states remains human-authorized.

Audit and Revision

Over time the system periodically revisits prior decisions.

Triggers for review may include:

  • periodic review schedules
  • conflicts between PIFRs
  • criteria expiration
  • deviations between expected and observed outcomes
  • newly available information

Audits trace reasoning lineage through the PIFR chain and compare past reasoning against current system state.

This enables:

  • reconstruction of decisions
  • detection of semantic drift
  • revision and repair

New insights then generate additional PIFRs, continuing the cycle.

The Learning Loop

  System Pressures

Research Context

The framework draws on several research traditions concerned with the stability of complex systems, including:

  • systems theory and cybernetics
  • institutional governance and political economy
  • knowledge representation and semantic infrastructure
  • AI accountability and decision provenance

These traditions provide context for understanding how evolving knowledge systems can remain interpretable and accountable under conditions of accelerating change.
